**Our Governance**

The Experimental Publishing Compendium is a community-led, not-for-profit, and values-driven resource that developed out of the [COPIM](https://copim.pub/projects/copim-2019-2023/) and [Open Book Futures](https://copim.pub/projects/open-book-futures-2023-2026/) projects. The Compendium is curated and maintained by an *Editorial Oversight Group *(EOG). The EOG consists of: 

- [Janneke Adema](https://openreflections.org/) (Coventry University) 
- [Simon Bowie](https://simonxix.com/) (University of Edinburgh)
- [Julien McHardy](https://www.matteringpress.org/about/mattering-people) (Mattering Press)
- [Whitney Trettien](https://www.english.upenn.edu/people/whitney-trettien) (University of Pennsylvania)
- [Simon Worthington](https://vivo.tib.eu/fis/display/n0000-0002-8579-9717) (Open Science Lab, TIB)

The EOG are advised and supported by a *Compendium Advisory Community*, which consists of representatives of some of the key stakeholders in experimental book publishing: academics or experimental publishing experts, not-for-profit open access presses, and open source tool and platform providers. The Compendium Advisory Community are: 

- Joe Deville (Open Book Collective)
- Mike Fortun (PECE)
- Remi Kalir (Duke University)
- Gimena del Rio Rande (CONICET)
- Terence Smyre (Manifold)
- Nick Thurston (University of Leeds)
- Niels-Oliver Walkowski (Melusina Press)
- Jane Winters (School of Advanced Studies, University of London)

The Experimental Publishing Compendium is closely connected and aligned to the [Open Book Collective ](https://openbookcollective.org/)(OBC), a UK-registered charity that enables libraries and institutions to financially support OA book publishers and service providers. A *Memorandum of Understanding* between the Compendium EOG and OBC was signed in March 2026 that puts in place a broad framework for collaboration and support between the Compendium and the OBC as they both grow and develop. This includes a commitment from the OBC to provide expertise, networks, and visibility for the Compendium to draw on, to forward both the Compendium’s and the OBC’s shared goals, and similarly for the EOG towards the OBC and its members. 

We are grateful to Lancaster University Library, who will take on the hosting of the server that the Experimental Publishing Compendium sits on now that the Open Book Futures project has come to an end.
